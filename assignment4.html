Challenge 5: Accessibility Concerns

I will be addressing an accessibility concern with challenge 2, the chess board. Someone who is blind and using an e-reader would not be able to view this chessboard effectively. Even if the reader could manually tell them "Black king E-5" and "White pawn b-3" it would be extremely difficult for them to memorize and understand that much information all at once pertaining to the pieces. Though, I do not think the e-reader would accurately read out the pieces since I don't know if it can understand unicode. This could be addressed by adding a short paragraph which lays out the details of the chess board instead of the e-reader trying to read out the table.
